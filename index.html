What I do

Stringed Instrument Repair

From 2010 to 2019 I apprenticed and worked with Scott Pack at Haggerty's Muscworks . In the summer of 2019 I attended the Violin Bow Restoration Workshop at Oberlin College, and for the entire year of 2019 plus 2 months I was the senior guitar tech (Hub Tech) for the new Guitar Center at Roosevelt Square in Seattle, Washington.

Mathematematics

I graduated from the South Dakota School of Mines & Technology with a BS in applied and computational mathematics in 2018. Since graduating I have spent a lot of time studying quantum mechanics on my own, forcing myself to complete all problems following each section in Liboff's Introductory Quantum Mechanics and getting to chapter 5 after a year of Sundays. Each section builds on the one before. On a few occasions, I would find out that I was kidding myself, and had to go back a chapter or two. This is high drama and addicting as any drug. I've discovered math is more fun without guard rails.

Computer programming + Web development

This is where I hope to begin to give back to humanity. I hunger to gain the knowledge required to build systems and to unlock valuable secrets hidden in data, and then comunicate to the masses. To that end, I have spent a lot of time learning front-end web development on freecodecamp.org and data science on dataquest.io. Both of these things have served to keep my education current--to update knowledge acquired in 2 semesters of Tech. Com. and several semesters of statistics and one semester of data analysis, all at SDSM&T.
