What I do

Stringed Instrument Repair

From 2010 to 2019 I apprenticed and worked with Scott Pack at Haggerty's Muscworks . In the summer of 2019 I attended the Violin Bow Restoration Workshop at Oberlin College, and for the entire year of 2019 plus 2 months I was the senior guitar tech (Hub Tech) for the new Guitar Center at Roosevelt Square in Seattle, Washington.

Mathematematics

I graduated from the South Dakota School of Mines & Technology with a BS in applied and computational mathematics in 2018. Course work included statistics, proof-based calculus, abstract algebra, mathematical modeling, numerical methods and complex analysis. Since graduating I've been spending a lot of time working problems out of a quantum mechanics text with the medium-term goal of getting into cloud-based quantum computing.

Computer programming + Web development

This is where I hope to give back. I hunger to gain the knowledge and skills required to glean hidden knowledge from data, and then comunicate to the masses. To that end, I have spent a lot of time learning front-end web development on freecodecamp.org and data science on dataquest.io. These online learning platforms have both expanded and kept current my college education.

Who I am

Developer based out of Seattle, WA

After dropping out of UNL in 2008, I moved to Rapid City, South Dakota, a town on the edge of the Black Hills. In 2010 I was working concessions for events at the local civic center and playing violin in the Black Hills Symphony Orchestra and the Jolly Llamas bluegrass band when I went into Haggerty's Musicworks and asked for a job.

Because of my connections to the music scene, both sacred and profane, I got the job selling violins, guitars, cellos, basses, violas, etc. A year into this job I was given my own work bench where I would repair stringed instruments full time under the guidance of Scott Pack. As the years went by the bar gigs started paying well enough for me to finish a math degree from the South Dakota School of Mines in 2018. Shortly after graduation, I voted for Billie Sutton for South Dakota governor, I quit my job, and I moved to Seattle.

I have a unique background and an interesting present as I look toward the future. Follow my progress!
