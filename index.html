What I do

Stringed Instrument Repair

From 2010 to 2019 I apprenticed and worked with Scott Pack at Haggerty's Muscworks repairing stringed instruments. In the summer of 2019 I attended the Violin Bow Restoration Workshop at Oberlin College, and for the entire year of 2019 plus 2 months I was the senior guitar tech (Hub Tech) for the new Guitar Center at Roosevelt Square in Seattle, Washington. Stringed instrument repair remains a passion and a side hustle.

My Work

Mathematematics

I graduated from the South Dakota School of Mines & Technology with a B.Sc. in applied and computational mathematics in 2018. Course work included statistics, proof-based calculus, abstract algebra, mathematical modeling, numerical methods and complex analysis. I learned a lot from my mentor and advisor, Roger Johnson.

Computer programming + Web development

This is where I hope to give back. To that end, I have spent a lot of time learning front-end web development on freecodecamp.org and data science on dataquest.io. These online learning platforms have both expanded and kept current my college education.

Who I am

Developer based out of Seattle, WA

I am a craftsman and a tinkerer. My desk regularly holds clutter consisting of the following:

  • Piles of paper containing calculations and notes
  • One too many 'vintage' Thinkpads running Debian
  • Wet stones and chissels and blades as sharp as a surgen's scalpal

It's fortunate that my little apartment contains two desks.
